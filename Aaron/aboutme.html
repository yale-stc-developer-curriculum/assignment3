When I was a little boy, I hid under tables and read books and got in trouble.

When I was a slightly bigger boy, I sat brazenly at my desk and read books and got in trouble.

When I was a teenager, I sat brazenly at my desk and read books and nobody cared; I was a lost cause.

Also when I was a teenager, I realized one could read on the Internet, in addition to playing Neopets.

So I started here, then added this and this, followed by this, which led me here, here and here, and so on and so forth.

When I came to college, I started writing. First came this, then this and this and many more besides.

Nowadays, I do many things. I'm the publisher of the Yale Record, a managing editor with the Yale Globalist, and a staff writer for the Yale Herald and Out of Order Magazine. I've also written for a dozen other Yale publications, plus a few grown-up places like the alumni magazine and Grist.

I've also been:

  • A project manager with the Yale Undergraduate Consulting Group
  • A teaching volunteer with New Haven Reads, Mathcounts Outreach, and Splash
  • A tutor with Revolution Prep and Bulldog Tutors
  • A research assistant in the Rudd Center and Yale's Clinical and Affective Neuroscience lab
  • The accidental pen-pal of a Chinese girl about to start college in America

I sing, dance, and act. I lift heavy objects for fun.

I plan to learn everything worth knowing.

Soon, I will be unstoppable.

But always humble!

Renewable Energy

Solar Panels

I say I'm a fan of renewable energy, and I am, but so far I have really just focused on solar photovoltaic. I want to know the science, politics, and economics of solar energy on a local, national, and international scale. I really want to promote solar energy, and that's actually what motivated me to do the YEI tech bootcamp program.

I found out about a company called SolarList through a friend, and one weekend, I went out into New Haven, knocked on people's doors, and took them through the SolarList web app that instantaneously provided a free estimate of solar potential and potential dollar savings. This gives people greater access to solar energy and lowers the cost of solar because it reduces solar companies' huge customer acquisition costs. I want to learn how to make websites and web apps to connect people in need of solar to people supplying it, just like SolarList is doing.

I am also currently working on promoting solar specifically at Yale through a group called Project Bright

At Yale

The Project Bright team already primed the Yale Farm for a solar thermal installation by determining the shading and solar potential of the roof of a Yale Farm structure. We are also preparing for an installation on the roof of the Kline Geology Lab. There's a lot more in store for next year, and I'm going to be working on developing killer solar training sessions, so stay up to date by checking out the Project Bright website.

If you share an interest or just want to say hi, feel free to email me at grissman@gmail.com.
